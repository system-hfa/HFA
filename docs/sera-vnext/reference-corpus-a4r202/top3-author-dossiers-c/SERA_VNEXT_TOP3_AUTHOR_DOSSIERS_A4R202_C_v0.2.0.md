### 3.1 Comair 5191

#### Operational summary

The crew taxied for runway 22, stopped at the runway 26 hold short line, accepted takeoff clearance while still short of runway 22, crossed the runway 26 hold short line, lined up on runway 26, and began takeoff from the wrong runway.

#### Candidate escape point statement

quando a tripulação cruzou a hold short line da pista 26 e alinhou a aeronave na pista 26 sem uma verificação positiva de posição e heading em relação à pista 22 autorizada.

#### Relevant facts

- Taxi clearance and readback were for runway 22.
- The airplane stopped at the runway 26 hold short line instead of the runway 22 hold short line.
- The first officer reported ready for departure while the aircraft was still at the wrong hold short point.
- The takeoff clearance exchange did not restate the runway number.
- The crew then crossed the runway 26 hold short line and completed lineup on runway 26.

#### Evidence in favor of the candidate escape point

- The stop point, the hold-short crossing, and the runway-26 lineup are concretely anchored in the factual record.
- The candidate point sits before takeoff acceleration and before downstream runway-overrun consequences.
- Position and heading verification duties were still live and recoverable at runway-entry time.

#### Evidence against or limitations

- A slightly earlier candidate remains defensible at the runway 26 hold short stop itself.
- Acceptance of takeoff clearance while still in the wrong place is also a plausible earlier candidate.
- First officer recall was unavailable, so the final missed cue chain remains partly reconstructed.

### 3.2 Asiana 214

#### Operational summary

The approach remained high, the PF selected `FLCH SPD`, disconnected the autopilot, moved the thrust levers to idle, and the autothrottle entered `HOLD` without integrated crew recognition, after which energy decayed through the late visual approach until the go-around attempt came too late.

#### Candidate escape point statement

quando o PF desconectou o autopilot, levou as thrust levers para idle e o A/T entrou em `HOLD` sem percepção integrada da tripulação, enquanto a aproximação continuava alta e exigia correções finas de energia e trajetória.

#### Relevant facts

- The airplane crossed DUYET and later 5 nm while still above the desired path.
- `FLCH SPD` was selected late in the approach and the autopilot was then disconnected.
- Thrust levers reached idle and the autothrottle transitioned to `HOLD`.
- No crewmember later recalled noticing the loss of automatic speed control.
- The 500 ft gate remained unstabilized and the go-around did not occur there.

#### Evidence in favor of the candidate escape point

- The automation-state transition and idle-thrust sequence are factually well anchored.
- The candidate point captures the boundary between being high and entering manual energy-management without integrated recognition.
- It sits before the late low-energy awareness around 200 ft and before impact consequences.

#### Evidence against or limitations

- The 500 ft gate remains a strong alternative because it provides an explicit stable-approach discontinuation boundary.
- Automation-state interpretation creates technical-boundary pressure and may pull review toward system logic instead of crew monitoring.
- The PM/instructor lane remains materially relevant, so actor attribution is not a single-person closure.

### 3.3 UPS 1354

#### Operational summary

The crew entered the nighttime nonprecision approach with the FMC still not properly resequenced, remained high, shifted from the briefed profile method to `vertical speed` without shared method closure, and continued a 1,500 fpm descent through minimums without an effective level-off.

#### Candidate escape point statement

quando a aproximação continuou com o FMC não resequenciado e o capitão migrou para `vertical speed` sem briefing compartilhado, mantendo descida de 1,500 fpm até e através da MDA sem level-off eficaz.

#### Relevant facts

- The aircraft was cleared for the approach while the FMC still remained direct-to-KBHM instead of properly sequenced.
- Once established on the localizer, the airplane remained high relative to the intended vertical profile.
- Near the FAF, the captain changed from profile logic to `vertical speed`.
- The first officer had to notice the `V/S` mode change after the fact.
- The descent continued at about 1,500 fpm through the MDA without the required minimums protection.

#### Evidence in favor of the candidate escape point

- The setup error, the unbriefed mode change, and the continued descent through minimums form a coherent factual chain.
- The candidate point preserves the boundary between setup error and continued unsafe descent inside the nonprecision segment.
- It sits before the late EGPWS alerts and before terrain impact consequences.

#### Evidence against or limitations

- An earlier candidate remains plausible at the failed FMC resequence stage alone.
- A later candidate remains plausible at the precise MDA continuation point.
- Procedure and automation can pull the review toward technical framing unless the direct cockpit action lane is kept central.

#### Evidence relevant to perception/objective/action, without classification
